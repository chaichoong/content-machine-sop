🎬 Stage A

Video Production — Intro + Subtitle

Join the Runpreneur intro to the start of an episode and burn captions onto the main video. Produces one finished MP4 ready to publish.

🎯 A1. Purpose & Scope

The Intro + Subtitle page joins the Runpreneur intro clip to the start of a recorded episode and burns word-for-word captions onto the main part of the video. It produces one finished MP4 ready to upload to YouTube and Google Drive.

Captions are made by Gemini, which listens to Kevin’s audio and writes out what he says. The captions then appear on screen at roughly the right time as Kevin speaks.

ℹ️
Scope: In scope — merging the intro clip, transcribing speech, burning subtitles, exporting a Full HD MP4. Out of scope — reframing 360° .insv footage (use the Insta360 Reframe page first), thumbnail design, social copywriting.

💡 A2. When to Use This Skill

💬 Trigger Phrases

Use this workflow when:

  • A new Runpreneur episode is recorded and ready for editing
  • The main video has been reframed from .insv to .mp4 (or recorded as MP4 already)
  • You need a Long Form Video for YouTube with a Runpreneur intro and burned-in subtitles
  • The AI Chain prompts you with “Intro + Subtitle — Active”
⚠️
Do not use this skill when: the video is still in .insv format (reframe first), the episode is a short-form clip without an intro, or the audio has no speech in it.

A1

Open the App and Pick the Page

manual
  1. Go to chaichoong.github.io/content-machine in Chrome.
  2. Look at the dark sidebar on the left.
  3. Click Intro + Subtitle. It is the page with the clapperboard icon.
  4. The page is highlighted orange when you are on it.
💡
If you started from the Pending Editing page and clicked “Long Form Video”, the AI Chain panel will appear on the right and walk you through this and the next steps.
A2

Upload the Main Video

manual
  1. Find the box that says “Drop main video or click to browse”.
  2. Drag your episode .mp4 file into the box. Or click the box and pick the file from your Mac.
  3. The video file name appears in blue under the box. Example: 1995 full.mp4 · 1147MB.
  4. Wait a few seconds for the video to load in the Preview panel on the right.
⚠️
If the file ends in .insv the upload is blocked. Open the Insta360 Reframe page in the sidebar first and turn the 360° file into a flat MP4. Then come back here.
A3

Mark the Insert Point

manual
  1. Click the orange Play button to start the video.
  2. Watch until Kevin finishes his opening line (usually 3–6 seconds in).
  3. Pause the video by clicking the canvas.
  4. Click Mark Insert Point at Current Time. A red pin drops on the timeline.
  5. You will see a confirmation like “Intro will be inserted at 0:05”.
ℹ️
The total time below the timeline now shows three numbers: Part 1 (before the intro), Intro (about 7 seconds), and Part 2 (after the intro to end). Total is the full length of the finished video.
A4

Tick Burn Subtitles into Export

manual
  1. Scroll down to the “Burn subtitles into export” tick box.
  2. Click the box so it shows a green tick.
  3. A new panel opens with the Gemini API Key field and a Transcribe button.
  4. The Gemini key is already saved. You should see dots in the field.
ℹ️
Subtitles only burn onto Part 1 and Part 2 (Kevin’s speech). The intro clip is left untouched because it has its own animation.
A5

Transcribe via Gemini

automated
  1. Click the purple “✨ Transcribe via Gemini” button.
  2. A status line appears showing chunk-by-chunk progress.
  3. You will see messages like “Chunk 3 / 28 (40s → 60s)…” and a 5-second countdown between chunks.
  4. Wait until the status turns green and says “Transcript ready — click Export to bake subtitles in”.
ℹ️
Why the 5-second pauses? Gemini’s free tier only allows 15 calls per minute. The pauses keep Kevin’s account safely under the limit. A 9-minute episode takes about 2–3 minutes to fully transcribe with pacing.
⚠️
If the status goes red and says “quota exceeded” or “RATE_LIMIT”, wait until midnight Pacific Time (about 8am UK) for the daily quota to reset and try again. Or open the Advanced section and add an OpenAI key as a fallback.
A6

Pick a Subtitle Style (optional)

manual
  1. Look just under the Transcribe button.
  2. There is a dropdown with three options: Orange pill (default), Dark pill, or Outline only.
  3. Drag the slider next to it to change font size.
  4. Watch the preview update on the right.
💡
Orange pill matches the Runpreneur brand colour. Stick with the default unless you have a reason to change it.
A7

Pick Output Resolution

manual
  1. Find the Output Resolution dropdown.
  2. Leave it on 1920×1080 (Full HD) for YouTube.
  3. Pick a lower resolution only if Kevin specifically asks for a smaller file.
A8

Export Combined MP4

automated
  1. Click the big orange “↓ Export Combined MP4” button.
  2. A progress bar appears showing how much of the export is done.
  3. For a 9-minute video, the export takes about 4–6 minutes.
  4. When finished, the browser asks you to save the file.
  5. Save it to your Downloads folder with the auto-suggested name (already includes the episode number).
⚠️
Do not close the Chrome tab during export. If you do, the export cancels and you have to start again.
A9

Upload to Google Drive and Save URL

manual
  1. Open Google Drive in a new tab.
  2. Drag the exported MP4 into the Runpreneur episode folder.
  3. Once uploaded, right-click the file and pick Get link.
  4. Make sure the link is set to Anyone with the link.
  5. Copy the link.
  6. Go back to Content Machine. Paste the link into the Paste Google Drive URL here… field in the AI Chain panel on the right.
  7. Click Save URL & Continue. This moves the AI Chain to the Thumbnail step.

📢 Stage B

Publishing & Sync

Publish the finished episode across all platforms, paste URLs back into Ready to Publish, set status to Approved, verify Runpreneur Sync, and update Strava.

🎯 B1. Purpose & Scope

This stage covers everything that happens AFTER an episode video is finished. It explains how Kevin publishes the episode across YouTube, blog, podcast, Facebook page, and social platforms. It also explains how the Runpreneur Sync page auto-updates the website with Stripe donation totals.

Some parts are automated by the Content Machine. Some parts are manual because Kevin prefers control over them, or because the platform does not allow automation.

ℹ️
Scope: In scope — Buffer scheduling, manual posting to YouTube/blog/podcast/Facebook, pasting URLs into Ready to Publish, setting episode status to Approved, Runpreneur Sync, manual Strava updates. Out of scope — video editing (see Stage A above), thumbnail creation, transcript generation.

💡 B2. When to Use This Skill

💬 Trigger Phrases

Use this stage when:

  • An episode MP4 has been exported from Stage A
  • The thumbnail is ready
  • Copywriting (caption, blog post, description) is approved
  • You are ready to publish the episode live across all platforms
⚠️
Do not use this stage when: the video is still being edited, the copywriting is not approved yet, or the thumbnail is missing. Publish only when everything is signed off.

B1

Schedule Social Posts in Buffer Account 1

manual
  1. Open Buffer at buffer.com and log in to Account 1.
  2. Click Create Post.
  3. Pick the platforms connected to this account (the free plan caps how many can connect to one Buffer account).
  4. Paste the approved caption from the Copywriting page.
  5. Attach the short-form clip or static image.
  6. Set the schedule time matching the Content Calendar slot.
  7. Click Schedule Post.
💡
Kevin uses TWO free Buffer accounts because the free plan limits how many channels one account can connect. Splitting platforms across two accounts is a free workaround for the paid plan.
B2

Schedule Social Posts in Buffer Account 2

manual
  1. Log out of Account 1, log in to Buffer Account 2.
  2. Click Create Post.
  3. Pick the platforms connected to this account (the remaining ones from Account 1).
  4. Paste the matching caption for each platform.
  5. Attach the right media.
  6. Set the schedule time.
  7. Click Schedule Post.
B3

Publish YouTube Full Video (Manual)

manual
  1. Open YouTube Studio.
  2. Click Create → Upload videos.
  3. Drag the finished MP4 into the upload box.
  4. Paste the title and description from the Copywriting page.
  5. Upload the custom thumbnail PNG.
  6. Set tags, category (People & Blogs), and visibility (Public or Scheduled).
  7. Click Publish (or schedule).
  8. Copy the public YouTube URL.
ℹ️
YouTube is NOT scheduled through Buffer because Buffer’s YouTube support is limited. Kevin uploads to YouTube Studio directly so he has full control over thumbnail, end screens, cards, and chapter markers.
B4

Publish Blog Post (Manual)

manual
  1. Open runpreneur.org.uk WordPress admin.
  2. Click Posts → Add New.
  3. Paste the blog title and body from the Copywriting page.
  4. Embed the YouTube video using the URL from B3.
  5. Set featured image to the thumbnail PNG.
  6. Add categories and tags (e.g. Runpreneur, Mindset, Daily Diary).
  7. Click Publish.
  8. Copy the blog post URL.
B5

Publish Podcast Episode (Manual)

manual
  1. Open the podcast host (e.g. Anchor, Buzzsprout, or whichever platform is in use).
  2. Click New Episode.
  3. Upload the audio file extracted from the MP4.
  4. Paste the podcast title and description from the Copywriting page.
  5. Set episode number and publish date.
  6. Click Publish.
  7. Copy the podcast episode URL.
ℹ️
The podcast platform handles distribution to Spotify, Apple Podcasts, and other directories automatically once published.
B6

Publish to Facebook Page (Manual)

manual
  1. Open the Runpreneur Facebook page directly (not through Buffer).
  2. Click Create Post.
  3. Paste the Facebook caption from the Copywriting page.
  4. Attach the video file or paste the YouTube link.
  5. Click Post.
  6. Copy the Facebook post URL.
ℹ️
Facebook page is published manually because Buffer’s Facebook support has changed and Kevin gets better reach posting natively.
B7

Paste All URLs into Ready to Publish

manual
  1. Go back to Content Machine → Ready to Publish page.
  2. Find the episode card for today’s episode.
  3. Paste the URLs collected in B3–B6 into the matching fields:
    • YouTube URL
    • Blog post URL
    • Podcast URL
    • Facebook post URL
  4. Click Save.
B8

Set Episode to Approved

approval gate
  1. Still on Ready to Publish, find the episode status dropdown.
  2. Change status from Pending to Approved.
  3. The episode moves to the Published Content page automatically.
  4. Done. Episode is live everywhere.
✅
Once Approved, the episode appears on the Stats page and counts toward the Runpreneur fundraising progress.
B9

Verify Runpreneur Sync (Automatic)

automated
  1. Open Content Machine → Runpreneur Sync page.
  2. Confirm the latest Stripe donation total has pulled through.
  3. Confirm the website shows the updated counter (visit runpreneur.org.uk).
  4. No manual sync needed — the page updates automatically.
ℹ️
If the website counter does NOT match Stripe within a few minutes, click Refresh Now on the Runpreneur Sync page to force a manual pull.
B10

Update Strava (Manual, Kevin Only)

manual
  1. After Kevin’s daily run, open strava.com.
  2. Manually update the run entry with distance, time, and any notes.
  3. Add the episode link or hashtag if relevant.
  4. Click Save.
⚠️
Kevin-only step. Strava has API rate limits and the AI workflow has its own caps that make full automation unreliable. Kevin prefers to update Strava himself because he is more comfortable doing this manually and it stays connected to his actual training.
